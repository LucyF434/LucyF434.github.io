Lucy Flanagan

I am a college student at New College Durham studying Games design. I have worked on environments, character design, promotional videos, my own games and many more projects throughout my time at college and in my spare time over the years.

I love to design my own concepts for objects and see them used in my own game with my own touch or experimenting in different ways whilst educating myself, which gives me a lot of power to design and express myself through my work


Environment


For this project we had to strictly stick to the SCI-FI theme and create our own environment which we would like to see in a future game of ours using the software Unreal Engine.


Due to it being my first time using Unreal engine and starting late in my course after transferring from a different college I spent extra time at college and home learning about the software and then applying it to my own work


I created my own concepts and ideas which can also be seen below I therefore followed to create an environment I felt strictly kept with the theme.


Environment trailer

we were given a short project where we had to design a trailer for our environment using camera angle rigging and music.


Character design

For this project we were given the objective to create our own designs in a SCI-FI theme that we would like to see in our environment. Each character had to have a certain agenda or use which therefore influenced my designs. I was given a month for this project and calculated my time carefully to ensure I would meet my deadline and recorded each step of my design process which can be seen here.



In these sketches I designed clothing which I saw fit for the characters, these designs vary in luminescent bright colours to more villainous colours such as red and purple. They are also built up of modern and futuristic shapes to seem bold and sometimes intimidating. These aspects have been used more in designs for main characters than in NPC characters.


B&Q Game 


the B&Q project I was given was a project inspired by the retail chain B&Q where you could experiment with your own talents and express your abilities with artistic freedom. There were guidelines we had to follow to make sure we would be appealing to the audience. we were given many options of ideas, the one idea I decided to go with was a platform runner game.


Pixel GIF


In preparation for my game I experimented with the app Procreate which I had used before briefly. I created the short clip shown here with a designed pixel character and background using Pinterest images as inspiration. This was my first time using procreate for animation and got to learn more about animation as a whole such as how to create smooth movement, timing and placement.


I later used the pixel characters movement in my game and its design


The making of my B&Q game

Whilst making my B&Q game I created various objects such as cardboard boxes, shelves, trollies, tools, etc for the background of my platform game which I felt would fit its theme and look like it was in a B&Q warehouse.


I created various alterations of the objects so that the background wasn't repetitive. I also added in obstacles for the player to jump over.


I also designed and created sprites for the lighting making them flicker and also further improved on the running animation used in the short clip and designed a jump animation too

Promotional video


the aim of this project was to create a promotional video for the college in our own style concentrating on what the students at New College think of their course, the college itself and how helpful it is along with the facilities it provides. we were given two months to produce this video and were allowed to use software such as photoshop and a camera.

for this project I was placed in a group of three where we each assigned roles for who was better suited to each task. I was in charge of the design ideas and creation of these designs. I also took part in the filming process around the campus and interview questions.


due to this project being centred around the games design course I felt it was right to have the design aspect fully represent it but also felt it was important to be visually pleasing, intriguing and fun. I designed 3 pixel character versions of me and my group with their own animations. these animations consisted of walking, stance, climbing and a welcome sign for the start of then video. I also voiced my own character with lines me and my group wrote


throughout this experience I was given the option to improve my design skills but also got to learn more from working in a group project which I will be able to use in the future of my career.
